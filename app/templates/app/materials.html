Common 3D printing materials are usually thermoplastics which means they dont degrade on application of heat. Materials also have varying properties which means one may be more suited to an application than another.

The following is a side by side comparison of the the two materials we specialize in and should help you decide which material to choose.

PETG

  • Firstly, and most importantly, it's Durable, it's more flexible than PLA but also a little softer. You'd have a hard job breaking it in half. So if an unbreakable case or enclosure is what you need, PETG trumps its competitor.
  • It has very low shrinkage, and therefore no warping. This is ideal for printing big stuff.
  • It has fantastic layer adhesion. This contributes to the strength of the print.
  • Great chemical resistance along with alkali, acid and water resistance.
  • It has a glass transition temperature of 80C which is worth taking into consideration.

PLA

  • PLA is hard but brittle. It is more likely to break rather than bend under loads but would have put up quite a decent fight before it does so.
  • It also has low shrinkage meaning it can print big stuff as well. Nothing to separate it from PETG here.
  • The finish that PLA produces is usually very neat. It can be easily pigmented meaning colors are vivid.
  • You dont need to feel bad when throwing out PLA because this material is easily biodegradable, made from natural ingredients.
  • It has a lower glass transition temperature than PETG so dont put it in high temperature situations. 60C is the recommended top limit.

So let's sum it all up for you

If your print is destined to be a vital mechanical part which will be undergoing loading or your part will be exposed to harsh environmental conditions, then PETG is definately the material to choose.

Alternatively, if your print is more artistically oriented or even mechanical but with minimal loads, PLA should be your go to material.

Generaly, PLA is suitable for most applications. Only when you feel the need for that extra strength and/or flexibility shoud you choose PETG.
